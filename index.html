GOÐAFOSS

Located in the north-east part of Iceland between Akureyri and Lake Mývatn. Its name translates to The Watefall of The Gods. The cascade is divided into two horseshoe-shaped falls.

SKÓGAFOSS

One of the biggest (60m drop) and one of the most popular waterfalls in Iceland. Located in the south of Iceland. It is believed that behind the waterfall there is a secret cave with a chest of gold.

SVARTIFOSS

It is surrounded by hexagonal dark basalt columns which gave rise to its name The Black Waterfall. Located in Vatnajökull National Park which is the largest national park in Europe.

SELJALANDSFOSS

Probably the most photographed waterfall in Iceland. Thanks to a wide cavern behind the waterfall, it is possible to fully encircle it. Located in the south of Iceland, 30 km from Skógafoss.

GULLFOSS

The Golden Falls is actually two separate waterfalls on the river Hvítá. One of the most powerful and beloved waterfalls in Iceland, it is a part of the famous Golden Circle route.

HRAUNFOSSAR

Lava Falls is a series of waterfalls that flow from underneath the lava field Hallmundarhraun formed in an eruption of one of the volcanoes lying under the Langjökull glacier.
